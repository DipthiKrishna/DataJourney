California Grizly Bear

The California grizzly bear was designated officially as California State Animal in 1953.

Scientific Name: Ursus californicus
Average life span: 20-30 yrs
Average size: 8 feet tall when standing.
Average weight: Females -aprx 400 pounds, Males -aprox 1,000 pounds.

As the state’s largest and fiercest predator, the grizzly had California to itself for hundreds of thousands of years. They could run 35 miles per hour for short distances, had a humped shoulder, and were brown-golden in color. The loss of habitat and over-hunting by a rapidly growing human population led to their complete extinction by the 1920s. In 1953, the California grizzly became the official State Animal and remains one of the state’s most enduring and visible symbols. Notably, California is the only state in the union that carries the image of an extinct animal on its state flag and seal.
